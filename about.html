Meditations on the Macro World

Artist Statement

It all started with a hike with my friend this semester. We went on a hike to 5th water hot springs in Spanish Fork Canyon. Some ways up the trail we left the path to inspect some decaying logs and this giant rock growing liken, moss and mushrooms. Most of the pictures were taken here. Some others were taken of stickers on the bridges and signs.

When taking the photos I chose to use my favorite lens, a Yashinon 50 mm f/1.7 with various macro extension tubes. Some of the pictures are actually like 20 pictures stitched together because when you are focusing so close on something not everything will be in focus and by taking 20 pictures I can make everything be in focus. Other of the pictures have a grainy texture, those were taken on film with a pink tint—Chosesn for cool artistic effect. Others of the pictures have a swirly bokeh—the blurry out of focus area of the picture—this was achieved by setting my aperture very wide between f1.7 and 2.8 depending on the shot, and using an imperfect vintage lens.

This work is a study of the small world looking at things we don’t usually see with our own eyes. One has to look close to see these things. How could we let people destroy the forest environments where these organisms live? Some of the species of moss include Syntrichia ruralis, and Forkmosses. Liken species include foliose lichen, Protoparmeliopsis, and others. As for the fungi, while it may look like there are macro fungi growing, they are actually more lichen (which is fungi technically) It's a species of fungi called cladonia or pixie cup lichen.

Yoshi Welch
